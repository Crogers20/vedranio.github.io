Why does this page look so shit?

So you're thinking 'You are a designer, why does this page look so shit?'. Well, I used to have a shiny, fancy-pants Wordpress theme page, which looked great, but something about it just didn't feel right. Every time I wanted to tweak something, it was too hard.

I decided to learn to write HTML, CSS and JavaScript from scratch. What you are seeing here are the very first baby steps I am taking in this journey.

I've written more about this on my blog.

Current work

Currently, I am working as a senior UX designer at Seek.com.au. More specifically, I am working on the advertiser side of the business, building products hirers and recruiters use in sourcing talent.

Day to day, I work closely with two agile teams and a couple of product managers. My work is a combination of forward looking activities (user research and journey mapping for future products on our roadmap), tactical delivery (designing and testing user flows and interfaces for the projects I am currently working on) as well as contributing to the improvement of processes and the way we design at Seek (e.g. defining a style guide).

Progress!

It doesn't look like much, but I've actually made a bit of progress.

The whole page is now styled using CSS. I have also learnt how to use Google web fonts (easy). The biggest achievement has been setting the whole page to a 30px baseline grid, to achieve vertical rythm. Turns out, this is a bit tricky using CSS.
