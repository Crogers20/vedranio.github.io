Visitor registration app

GymSales are a provider of lead and member management software for gyms and fitness centres. They wanted to extend their product set to include an iPad application for management of visitor registrations.

The challenge

Gym receptions are busy places. Multiple instructors are meeting their students, loud music is playing, phones are ringing…

GymSales wanted to provide gym visitors with a quick and painless way to register as visitors, while meeting gyms‘ data and legal requirements. Therein lied the biggest challenge for the project: how to collect all the data required, while keeping the process of registering quick and simple?

Research

I hit the streets and walked to three different gyms where I signed up as a visitor in order to experience the registration process. The main learnings were:

  • They all used paper forms
  • The forms often did not allow enough space for the required information
  • The space allocated to completion of forms was inadequate and uncomforable
  • The amount of information asked for was overwhelming.

Simplify from the start

Before starting any design, I talk to the client about minimising the amount of inofmation we collect.

The principle we ended up agreeing on is that if the information was not required during the 'visitor' period, we did not need to collect it with this app. Any information that was required for full members would be collected once (if) they signed up as full members.

Animation and the perception of effortlessness

I was keen for the process of registering to feel as effortless as possible. Once the amount of information was minimised, I turned to animation design in order to come up with screen transitions which gave the impression of speed, smooth movement and progress.

Elements logically morph into each other

Objects don’t just appear on the screen. They enter and leave the screen in an orchestrated manner.

Movement of individual elements overlaps by a 0.1s, in order to create a sense of smooth continuity

All animations used an ease-in/ease-out curve with the exception of error messages – error messages spring onto the screen in order to draw attention.

Oversized input controls

From my research, I knew that the existing forms were physically cramped and difficult to fill out – I didn't want this application to feel like a form, hence I opted to use large, oversized elements, such as the input fields, action buttons and links.

Usability testing

I improvised in order to recruit subjects for my usability testing – I reached out to friends and acquaintances who joined a gym within the last year. I managed to find four people very quickly and tested the app prototype.

The key change made after the usability testing was to provide explanation as to why the phone number was needed – some people were uncomfortable with providing this information.

Design handoff

The developers I worked with on this app were located in Russia. I wanted to make sure that none of the subtleties of the animation I designed were lost in the handoff process. I came up with a way of documenting the animation details, which ensured that the final product closely resembled my design.

The outcome

The app was finished in May 2016 and is due to be rolled out to 100s of gyms around the USA in the coming months.
